Create a modern, professional, mobile-friendly website for a photobooth rental company that offers hourly service packages based on event needs and add-on options. The website should feel fun, stylish, and trustworthy, with a clean layout that highlights the company’s services, pricing, and past work. Include strong call-to-action buttons like “Request a Quote,” “View Packages,” and “Contact Us” throughout the site. Photobooth sites commonly benefit from clear service pages, visible pricing, strong visuals, and an easy contact form.

Website goals
Showcase photobooth service packages by hour.

Display pricing clearly for each package.

Show add-ons such as props, backdrop options, physical prints, digital copies, and custom branding.

Include an inquiry/request section where visitors can send questions or requests through a form that emails the company.

Feature a gallery of previous event photos and booth setups to build trust and attract bookings. Strong image-based sections are especially important for photobooth websites.

Pages and sections to include
Home page with a bold hero section, short intro, and booking CTA.

Packages section with at least three tiers, such as Basic, Standard, and Premium.

Add-ons section for extras like props, custom backdrop, digital sharing, physical prints, album, and extra hours.

Gallery section with high-quality images of previous work.

About section describing the company and service quality.

Request a Quote / Contact section with a form that sends email inquiries to the company.

FAQ section answering common questions about setup, timing, delivery, and customization.

Package layout
Create a pricing section that shows each package in a separate card or column. Each package should list:

Hourly duration.

Price.

Included services.

Add-ons available.

Best use case, such as birthdays, weddings, corporate events, or private parties.

A simple three-tier package structure is effective because it gives customers options without overwhelming them, and hourly add-ons like extra time and custom backdrops are common in this market.

Contact/request form
Include a request or questions form that collects:

Full name.

Email address.

Phone number.

Event type.

Event date.

Event location.

Number of hours needed.

Package interest.

Services requested.

Additional notes or questions.

The form should be easy to find in the main navigation and should route inquiries by email. Best practices suggest keeping the form simple, making contact information visible, and including both a form and direct contact details.

Design style
Use a bright, elegant, event-friendly design with:

Clean typography.

Large attractive photos.

Smooth scrolling sections.

Soft shadows or rounded cards.

A color palette that feels festive but professional.

Responsive layout for mobile and desktop.

Example content tone
Write the website copy in a friendly, confident, and welcoming tone. Emphasize creating memorable experiences, high-quality photos, and easy booking for all kinds of events.
